The Problem

When seeing is no longer believing.

In 2024, a deepfake of a CFO on a Zoom call convinced an employee to wire $25 million to criminals. It will happen to a thousand companies this year. The digital world has lost its grip on truth.

Journalism in crisis

Newsrooms cannot prove their images are authentic. Editorial trust is collapsing in the age of AI-generated news.

Insurance fraud wave

AI-generated claim photos cost European insurers over 4 billion euros per year. Every doctored image is a payout.

Evidence under attack

Courts reject video footage that cannot be authenticated. Justice stalls when evidence cannot be trusted.

Executive impersonation

Deepfake Zoom calls impersonating CEOs have already drained tens of millions from global companies.

Marketplace chaos

Vinted, Leboncoin and eBay are flooded with AI-generated product photos. Buyers lose confidence every day.

Gen Z disbelief

67 percent of users under 25 no longer trust what they see online. A generation is ready for a new signal of truth.

FAQ

Hard questions, straight answers.

Why will not Adobe or Google just crush you with their own version?

They are building the standard (C2PA), not the consumer product. PROVE is the beautiful, neutral layer on top. Adobe owns Photoshop, we own the trust signal everywhere else.

What stops someone from just screenshotting to strip the signature?

Every verified file is also indexed on our ledger with a perceptual fingerprint. A clean screenshot still matches, and the provenance surfaces on verification.

Is this not just a feature, rather than a company?

SSL certificates were a feature once. Today they are a two billion dollar industry. Trust infrastructure always becomes a standalone category.

How do you handle detection if AI keeps improving?

We do not compete in detection. We authenticate what is real. The arms race is theirs. The ledger of truth is ours.

What about privacy and GDPR?

Signatures are device-based and optional. All personal data stays in the EU. Users can revoke signatures at any time. PROVE is GDPR compliant by design.
